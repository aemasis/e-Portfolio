Projects

Coursework, research projects, and applied work across library science, history of the book, and information design.

Artifact 6

Textile History

A subject guide created for LIS 702 on facilitating user experience. This webpage acts as an interactive subject guide providing users with eight books and four websites to begin exploring textile and material history. The books range from pop history to academic editions to give users different levels of information. The websites link to sites with robust digital collections and further resources to explore.

MLIS Student Learning Goals and Outcomes

This artifact reflects Goals 3 and 4 by demonstrating the ability to design a resource that facilitates access to information for a specific user community. The subject guide required evaluating and selecting a range of materials — from popular histories to academic texts — and organizing them in a way that supports different levels of research, reflecting Outcomes 3a and 3c. It also reflects Outcome 4c by applying principles of user learning and information literacy instruction to guide patrons through a subject area.

Artifact 7

Design Thinking Project

A design thinking proposal developed for LIS 707 imagining a new public programming initiative at the Huntington Library in San Marino, California. The project proposes a structured presentation series giving urban planning, architecture, and landscape architecture students and professionals hands-on access to the Huntington's architecture and photography collection — drawing on experience from the Newberry Library's similar public programming model.

LIS 707  ·  Design Thinking Project  ·  Amanda Masis ↓ Download PDF
MLIS Student Learning Goals and Outcomes

This artifact reflects Goals 3, 4, and 5 by proposing a new public programming initiative that would expand access to special collections material at the Huntington Library. It engages Outcome 3a by designing a resource and access model tailored to a specific professional community, and Outcomes 4a and 4c by applying library and information science principles around access, community engagement, and instructional design to a real institutional context. The proposal also reflects Outcomes 5a and 5b by demonstrating advocacy for broader public access to archival collections and articulating a clear, evidence-based case for the program's value.

Artifact 8

Mis-en-Page Analyses

Close formal analyses of historical manuscripts and objects from the History of the Book course, examining how physical layout, annotation, and material construction convey meaning. Objects analyzed include a Late Babylonian clay tablet containing Ludlul bēl Nēmeqi (c. 1307–1282 BC, British Museum) and an illuminated leaf from the Bible Historiale (1360–1370, J. Paul Getty Museum).

History of the Book  ·  Mis-en-Page Analyses  ·  Amanda Masis ↓ Download PDF
MLIS Student Learning Goals and Outcomes

This artifact reflects Goals 2, 3, and 4 by applying close formal analysis to historical manuscript objects, examining how physical layout, material construction, and paratextual elements shape the transmission and reception of information across time. It engages Outcome 2a by exploring how different material formats define and communicate information differently, and Outcome 3b by utilizing scholarly tools and methods to interpret and manage knowledge embedded in rare objects. The project also reflects Outcome 4a by connecting archival and book history theory to the direct analysis of primary source material.

Artifact 9

Book Conservation and Binding

An object examination report and treatment proposal for Poetry Loves Poetry: An Anthology of Los Angeles Poets (1985), edited by Bill Mohr and published by Momentum Press, Santa Monica. The book was purchased at The Last Book Store in Los Angeles in fair condition and is significant both for its contents — capturing the lived experience of Los Angeles in the 1970s and 80s — and as an artifact of the city's independent literary press history. The treatment plan proposes a transition from softcover to hardcover with a cover inset preserving the original covers, and a double fan adhesive bind for the text block.

LIS 804  ·  Object Examination & Treatment Proposal  ·  Amanda Masis ↓ Download PDF
MLIS Student Learning Goals and Outcomes

This artifact reflects Goals 3 and 4 by applying conservation theory and bookbinding practice to the physical preservation and treatment of a damaged object with cultural and personal significance. It engages Outcomes 3b and 3c by utilizing and evaluating conservation methods — including double fan adhesive binding and cover inset techniques — to determine the most appropriate and durable treatment for the text block and case. The project also reflects Outcome 4a by grounding treatment decisions in archival principles around preservation, provenance, and the balance between restoration and original material integrity.
