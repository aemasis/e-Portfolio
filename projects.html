Artifact 5

Textile History

A subject guide created for LIS 702 on facilitating user experience. This webpage acts as an interactive subject guide providing users with eight books and four websites to begin exploring textile and material history. The books range from pop history to academic editions to give users different levels of information. The websites link to sites with robust digital collections and further resources to explore.

Artifact 7

Design Thinking Project

A design thinking proposal developed for LIS 707 imagining a new public programming initiative at the Huntington Library in San Marino, California. The project proposes a structured presentation series giving urban planning, architecture, and landscape architecture students and professionals hands-on access to the Huntington's architecture and photography collection — drawing on experience from the Newberry Library's similar public programming model.

Artifact 8

Mis-en-Page Analyses

Close formal analyses of historical manuscripts and objects from the History of the Book course, examining how physical layout, annotation, and material construction convey meaning. Objects analyzed include a Late Babylonian clay tablet containing Ludlul bēl Nēmeqi (c. 1307–1282 BC, British Museum) and an illuminated leaf from the Bible Historiale (1360–1370, J. Paul Getty Museum).

Artifact 9

Book Conservation and Binding

An object examination report and treatment proposal for Poetry Loves Poetry: An Anthology of Los Angeles Poets (1985), edited by Bill Mohr and published by Momentum Press, Santa Monica.The treatment plan proposes a transition from softcover to hardcover with a cover inset preserving the original covers, and a double fan adhesive bind for the text block.
